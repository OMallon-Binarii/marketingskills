## Problem Agitation Section

**Section title:** Incidents don’t just expose data. They stall the business.

**Narrative copy:**
At 2:14 AM, suspicious activity alerts begin flooding your SOC dashboard. By 3:00 AM, incident response confirms ransomware behavior in a primary cloud environment. Access controls are tightened, systems are isolated, and teams start containment.

By 5:30 AM, the real business impact becomes clear: operations teams cannot access key unstructured files, clinical teams lose confidence in data integrity, support workflows slow, and executives ask the same question every minute: *Can we keep operating safely today?*

Traditional controls help detect and investigate. They do not guarantee that complete files remain inaccessible to attackers while still available to authorized teams under pressure.

**Transition line:** In a real incident, continuity is a security outcome.

## What Breaks During an Incident

**Section title:** Where most organizations lose operational control

- **File availability drops at the worst moment** when primary storage is locked, quarantined, or offline.
- **Clinical and operational workflows slow down** because teams cannot reliably retrieve trusted documents.
- **Breach blast radius expands** when compromised environments still contain complete, usable files.
- **Data exposure assessments become chaotic** because security teams cannot quickly prove what was accessed.
- **Executive decision-making degrades** without immutable, defensible evidence of file-level activity.
- **Regulatory pressure intensifies** as reporting timelines begin before technical clarity is established.

## Solution Section (Binarii Labs Approach)

**Section title:** Architect for continuity before an incident, not during one

**Body copy:**
Binarii Labs takes a prevention-first approach to unstructured data security. Instead of relying on perimeter controls to protect whole files, we ensure no single environment ever holds a complete file.

Every protected file is automatically:
1. **Duplicated** for resilience
2. **Encrypted** for confidentiality
3. **Fragmented** to remove full-file exposure in any one location
4. **Distributed** across at least three cloud locations your organization owns and controls

If one environment is breached, isolated, or unavailable, attackers see unusable encrypted fragments while authorized users maintain operational access through intact distributed architecture.

**Support statement:** You contain incidents without forcing business or clinical operations to stop.

## Objection Handling / FAQ

**1) Does this replace our existing DLP, SIEM, and SOC processes?**  
No. Binarii complements detection and response tools by reducing full-file exposure at the data architecture layer.

**2) What happens if one of our cloud providers is breached or unavailable?**  
Because no single provider stores a complete file, a single compromised location does not expose usable data or force full operational stoppage.

**3) Will this disrupt clinical workflows or user productivity?**  
Binarii is designed for low user friction. Protection is applied at the architecture layer without requiring constant end-user policy management.

**4) Can we keep control over where data resides?**  
Yes. Your organization selects and controls the cloud locations used for distribution.

**5) How does this help during ransomware specifically?**  
Ransomware often succeeds by locking or corrupting accessible data stores. Binarii reduces impact by preventing complete-file concentration in any single environment.

**6) How do we prove what happened for auditors and regulators?**  
Immutable Proof of Record provides time-stamped, tamper-resistant evidence of file activity for investigations, compliance reporting, and governance review.
